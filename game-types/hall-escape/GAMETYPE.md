# Game Type: Hall Escape

## Overview

Interior escape room in a large open space (50+ m²). Uses projector, audio system, and physical props. Designed for simultaneous teams competing or collaborating.

## Constraints

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Space | Indoor, 50+ m² minimum |
| Players per team | 5–10 (optimal: 6–8) |
| Duration | 60–90 minutes |
| Technology | Projector, surround audio, optional tablets |
| Staff | Zero GM (self-service) |

## Recommended Mechanics

| Priority | Mechanics |
|---|---|
| Core | mecanismo, panel-electrico, puzzle-ensamblaje, busqueda-objetos |
| Supporting | nonogram, secuencial, memoria, exploracion-visual |
| Digital | tablet-cooperativo, panel-control-app |
| Avoid | gps-navegacion, ubicacion-qr (outdoor-only) |

## Flow Structure

- **multi_lineal** preferred: teams split across simultaneous puzzle stations
- **lineal** acceptable for smaller spaces or narrative-driven games
- Timer displayed on projector; ambient audio sets atmosphere

## Design Adaptations

- Projector can display puzzle interfaces, maps, or narrative elements
- Audio channels can deliver hints or ambient cues
- Physical stations must be clearly delineated (tape, rugs, furniture)
- Multiple copies of shared documents needed (one per team)
- Anti-cheat: teams must not overhear or observe other teams' solutions
- All puzzles must work with 5+ players (no bottleneck puzzles for 1–2)

## Validation Checklist

- [ ] Space is 50+ m² with clear sight lines to projector
- [ ] Audio system covers all puzzle stations evenly
- [ ] Each team has dedicated station area with clear boundaries
- [ ] Timer and scoreboard visible to all teams
- [ ] No outdoor elements (GPS, QR on buildings, street navigation)
- [ ] Puzzle materials scale to team count (not individual)
- [ ] Solution verification is self-service (no GM needed)
- [ ] Anti-cheat: no team can short-circuit by observing another
