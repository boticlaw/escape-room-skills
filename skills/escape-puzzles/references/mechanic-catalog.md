# Mechanic Catalog — Detailed Descriptions

## Category: Logica

### nonogram

**Description**: Players fill a grid based on number clues to reveal a hidden image or code. Classic logic puzzle with visual payoff.

**Difficulty**: 3–7 | **Players**: 1–3 | **Duration**: 5–10 min

**When to use**: Need a puzzle that rewards logical deduction with a satisfying visual reveal. Good for mid-game difficulty ramp.

**Common errors**:
- Grid too large (>10×10) for the time budget
- Ambiguous solution (multiple valid fills)
- No connection between the revealed image and the narrative

**Scaling**:
- Easy: 5×5 grid, simple image, row-only clues
- Medium: 7×7 grid, color nonogram
- Hard: 10×10 grid, multi-color, time pressure

### posiciones

**Description**: Players arrange items (objects, images, words) in the correct positions based on relational clues. "The key is left of the lamp, right of the book..."

**Difficulty**: 2–5 | **Players**: 1–4 | **Duration**: 4–8 min

**When to use**: Spatial reasoning, narrative clues translated to physical arrangement. Works with physical props or printed boards.

**Common errors**:
- Ambiguous spatial language (which "left"?)
- Too many items (>6) creates combinatorial explosion
- Clues don't uniquely determine the arrangement

**Scaling**:
- Easy: 4 items, 3 explicit clues
- Medium: 5 items, relational clues
- Hard: 6 items, negative clues ("NOT next to")

### secuencial

**Description**: Players determine the correct order of elements (numbers, symbols, events) using pattern recognition or logical rules.

**Difficulty**: 2–6 | **Players**: 1–3 | **Duration**: 3–7 min

**When to use**: When you need a quick logic challenge that connects to narrative sequence (timeline, recipe steps, ritual order).

**Common errors**:
- Multiple valid orderings
- Pattern is too obscure or culturally specific
- No feedback until the complete sequence is entered

**Scaling**:
- Easy: 4 elements, obvious pattern (1-2-3-4)
- Medium: 5–6 elements, hidden pattern
- Hard: 7+ elements, multi-rule pattern

## Category: Emparejamiento (Matching)

### memoria

**Description**: Classic memory/matching game. Players reveal hidden items and find matching pairs. Tests short-term memory.

**Difficulty**: 1–4 | **Players**: 1–6 | **Duration**: 3–8 min

**When to use**: Quick, accessible puzzle for early game. Good for team bonding (shared memory). Works as an icebreaker.

**Common errors**:
- Too many pairs (>8) causes fatigue
- Images too similar (frustrating, not challenging)
- No narrative wrapper (feels like a kids' game)

**Scaling**:
- Easy: 4 pairs, distinct images
- Medium: 6 pairs, thematic images
- Hard: 8 pairs, similar categories

### texto

**Description**: Players match text fragments, translations, or descriptions to their corresponding items. Connects narrative to objects.

**Difficulty**: 2–5 | **Players**: 1–4 | **Duration**: 4–8 min

**When to use**: When you need to connect story documents to physical objects. Excellent for investigation games (match evidence to suspect).

**Common errors**:
- Multiple valid matches for one item
- Text fragments too similar
- No way to verify partial progress

**Scaling**:
- Easy: 3–4 pairs, direct descriptions
- Medium: 5–6 pairs, indirect descriptions
- Hard: 7+ pairs, deceptive descriptions with red herrings

## Category: Fisica (Physical)

### mecanismo

**Description**: Players interact with a physical mechanism — turning dials, sliding panels, rotating rings — to reach a target configuration.

**Difficulty**: 3–7 | **Players**: 1–3 | **Duration**: 5–12 min

**When to use**: When you want tangible, tactile interaction. Creates strong "aha" moments when the mechanism clicks into place.

**Common errors**:
- Mechanism is fragile or jams under player use
- No indication of progress (is this doing anything?)
- Requires force instead of finesse
- Not accessible to players with limited dexterity

**Scaling**:
- Easy: Single dial with clear markers
- Medium: Two interconnected mechanisms
- Hard: Three+ mechanisms with interdependent states

### panel-electrico

**Description**: Players connect wires, flip switches, or route circuits on a control panel to activate something (light, lock, display).

**Difficulty**: 3–8 | **Players**: 1–3 | **Duration**: 5–15 min

**When to use**: Industrial, laboratory, or military themes. High visual payoff when circuits complete. Great for hall escape setups.

**Common errors**:
- Color-only clues (not color-blind accessible)
- No visual feedback during partial completion
- Too many wires/switches (>6) without structure
- Real electrical hazard risk — use safe voltages only

**Scaling**:
- Easy: 3 wire pairs, color-coded
- Medium: 4–5 switches with logic conditions
- Hard: 6+ components with sequential dependency

### punteria-derribo

**Description**: Players aim and throw/knock/shoot to hit targets. Physical skill challenge (toss rings, knock cans, shoot targets).

**Difficulty**: 2–6 | **Players**: 1–4 | **Duration**: 3–8 min

**When to use**: Carnival, fairground, or action themes. Adds physical energy break between mental puzzles. Hall escape only.

**Common errors**:
- Inconsistent difficulty based on player height/strength
- Not accessible to all players
- Targets can break or be lost
- Too easy (no challenge) or too hard (frustrating)

**Scaling**:
- Easy: Large targets, close range, forgiving
- Medium: Smaller targets, pattern reveal on hit
- Hard: Moving targets, limited ammunition

### puzzle-ensamblaje

**Description**: Players assemble physical pieces into a complete object — jigsaw, 3D model, torn document, or mechanical assembly.

**Difficulty**: 2–6 | **Players**: 2–6 | **Duration**: 5–15 min

**When to use**: When you want collaborative, tangible problem-solving. Excellent for team building. Works in any theme with appropriate prop design.

**Common errors**:
- Pieces too similar (frustration without logic)
- No way to verify partial assembly
- Missing pieces (game-breaking)
- Assembly is tedious, not puzzling

**Scaling**:
- Easy: 6–10 pieces, distinct shapes
- Medium: 12–20 pieces, similar shapes with image clues
- Hard: 20+ pieces, abstract or incomplete reference image

## Category: Digital

### tablet-cooperativo

**Description**: Players split across two or more tablets with asymmetric information. They must communicate to solve a shared puzzle.

**Difficulty**: 3–7 | **Players**: 2–6 (split across devices) | **Duration**: 5–12 min

**When to use**: When you want to force communication between players. Each tablet shows different information that must be combined verbally.

**Common errors**:
- One player can solve both sides by looking at both screens
- Information is too complex to communicate verbally
- No progress indicator
- Tablet runs out of battery during game

**Scaling**:
- Easy: 2 tablets, simple visual match
- Medium: 2–3 tablets, coded information
- Hard: 3+ tablets, sequential dependencies, time pressure

### panel-control-app

**Description**: A central app/control panel that players interact with to manage game state — entering codes, activating systems, tracking progress.

**Difficulty**: 2–5 | **Players**: 1–4 | **Duration**: Continuous (used throughout game)

**When to use**: As the game's backbone — receives solutions from other puzzles, tracks progress, delivers narrative content and hints.

**Common errors**:
- App crashes or is unresponsive
- Input field doesn't accept the correct format
- No offline fallback
- Too much screen time — players should look at the room, not the app

**Scaling**:
- Easy: Simple code entry, progress bar
- Medium: Multiple input types, narrative reveals
- Hard: Real-time events, multiple concurrent tasks

### arcade-digital

**Description**: A mini-game on a screen that players must beat (or reach a score threshold) to proceed. Pac-Man, snake, breakout, etc.

**Difficulty**: 3–8 | **Players**: 1–2 | **Duration**: 3–10 min

**When to use**: Fun energy break between puzzles. Themed to match the game (space invaders for sci-fi, etc.).

**Common errors**:
- Game is too hard for non-gamers
- No way to skip after repeated failures
- Controls are confusing
- Breaks immersion if the game style doesn't match the theme

**Scaling**:
- Easy: Simple game, low score threshold
- Medium: Moderate difficulty, specific score target
- Hard: Complex game, high threshold, limited lives

### laberinto-digital

**Description**: Players navigate a digital maze on a screen. May require multiple players to cooperate (one sees the map, one drives).

**Difficulty**: 3–7 | **Players**: 1–4 | **Duration**: 3–10 min

**When to use**: Navigation-themed puzzles, space/sci-fi settings, or when you need an asymmetric cooperative challenge.

**Common errors**:
- Maze too large (tedious, not puzzling)
- No sense of direction or progress
- One player does everything
- Motion sickness with first-person view

**Scaling**:
- Easy: Small 5×5 maze, overhead view
- Medium: 8×8 maze, limited visibility
- Hard: 10×10+ maze, first-person, fog of war

## Category: Cooperativo (Cooperative)

### comunicacion-mensajes

**Description**: Players are separated (physically or by information) and must communicate via limited channels — walkie-talkie, written notes through a slot, or one-way messaging.

**Difficulty**: 3–7 | **Players**: 3–8 (split into 2+ groups) | **Duration**: 5–12 min

**When to use**: When you want to force teamwork and communication. Players have different pieces of information that must be shared.

**Common errors**:
- Communication channel too restrictive (impossible to convey info)
- Communication channel too open (one side does all the work)
- Time is spent on logistics, not puzzle-solving
- Players can bypass by shouting across rooms

**Scaling**:
- Easy: Two groups, simple descriptions, open channel
- Medium: Two groups, coded information, limited messages
- Hard: Three+ groups, sequential dependencies, restricted channel

## Category: Busqueda/Ubicacion (Search & Location)

### busqueda-objetos

**Description**: Players search the environment for hidden objects — under furniture, inside containers, behind panels. Classic escape room mechanic.

**Difficulty**: 1–3 | **Players**: All | **Duration**: 3–8 min

**When to use**: Early game warm-up, or as a connector between puzzles (find the key, find the document). Universally accessible.

**Common errors**:
- Objects hidden too well (players give up)
- Objects found too early by accident (skip puzzle sequence)
- No limit on search area (players waste time in wrong places)
- Objects can be lost or taken by previous groups

**Scaling**:
- Easy: 2–3 objects in obvious containers
- Medium: 4–5 objects, some hidden behind/under things
- Hard: 5+ objects, camouflaged, some are red herrings

### exploracion-visual

**Description**: Players examine their environment visually to find hidden information — UV text, hidden symbols, patterns in wallpaper, anomalies.

**Difficulty**: 2–5 | **Players**: All | **Duration**: 3–8 min

**When to use**: When you want players to engage with the environment and notice details. Rewards observation skills.

**Common errors**:
- Hidden element is too subtle (no one ever finds it)
- Hidden element is too obvious (no challenge)
- Requires specific lighting conditions that vary
- Depends on color vision only

**Scaling**:
- Easy: One obvious anomaly in a clear view
- Medium: Multiple elements, some require tools (UV light, magnifier)
- Hard: Pattern recognition across multiple surfaces

### ubicacion-qr

**Description**: Players scan QR codes at physical locations to receive puzzle content, clues, or validation. Outdoor/urban core mechanic.

**Difficulty**: 1–3 | **Players**: All | **Duration**: 2–5 min per location

**When to use**: Street escape games. QR codes are the delivery mechanism for location-specific content.

**Common errors**:
- QR code damaged or unreadable
- Wrong QR code at location (after replacement)
- Players scan random QR codes not part of the game
- No internet for QR content delivery

**Scaling**:
- Easy: One QR, direct content
- Medium: Sequential QRs, each reveals next location
- Hard: Multiple QRs, must choose correct one based on context

### gps-navegacion

**Description**: Players navigate to GPS coordinates to unlock content or find the next location. Core mechanic for outdoor games.

**Difficulty**: 2–4 | **Players**: All | **Duration**: 5–10 min per waypoint

**When to use**: Street escape exclusively. Players move through real-world locations guided by GPS.

**Common errors**:
- GPS accuracy insufficient (10m+ error)
- Location is on private property or dangerous
- Weather makes the route unpleasant
- Players can Google the destination instead of navigating

**Scaling**:
- Easy: Direct coordinates, 100m radius
- Medium: Coordinates + riddle to find exact spot
- Hard: Multi-step navigation, intermediate waypoints

### acrostico-ubicacion

**Description**: Players collect letters from multiple locations that form a word when read in order. Location-based acrostic.

**Difficulty**: 2–5 | **Players**: All | **Duration**: Varies by location count

**When to use**: When you want to connect multiple locations into a single meta-puzzle. Each stop contributes one piece.

**Common errors**:
- Letter order is ambiguous
- One location is inaccessible or hard to find
- Word is too short (<4 letters) or too long (>8)
- Players can guess the word without visiting all locations

**Scaling**:
- Easy: 4 locations, common word
- Medium: 5–6 locations, thematic word
- Hard: 7+ locations, phrase or code

### adivinanza-ubicacion

**Description**: Players solve a riddle that describes a location. The answer is a physical place they must find and go to.

**Difficulty**: 3–6 | **Players**: All | **Duration**: 3–8 min

**When to use**: When you want to connect narrative to real-world locations. Riddles about landmarks, streets, or features.

**Common errors**:
- Riddle is ambiguous (multiple valid locations)
- Location only known to locals (unfair for visitors)
- Location changed or no longer exists
- Riddle is too easy (obvious from first line)

**Scaling**:
- Easy: Descriptive riddle, well-known location
- Medium: Metaphorical riddle, moderate familiarity needed
- Hard: Cryptic riddle, requires local knowledge or research

## Category: Investigacion (Investigation)

### investigacion-texto

**Description**: Players read and analyze documents (letters, reports, newspapers) to extract critical information. Cross-reference multiple sources to find contradictions or hidden truths.

**Difficulty**: 3–8 | **Players**: 1–6 | **Duration**: 5–15 min

**When to use**: Investigation and narrative-heavy games. Core mechanic for detective themes. Rewards careful reading and cross-referencing.

**Common errors**:
- Too much text (>1 page per document) causes fatigue
- Critical info buried in walls of text
- Documents are confusing rather than puzzling
- Only one player reads while others wait
- Contradictions are too subtle to notice

**Scaling**:
- Easy: 2–3 short documents, clear contradiction
- Medium: 4–5 documents, cross-reference needed
- Hard: 6+ documents, subtle clues, red herrings, timeline reconstruction
